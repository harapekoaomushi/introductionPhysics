Question on Rigid Rotor

Problem 1

A rigid body with moment of inertia of $Iz$ rotates freely in the $x$-$y$ plane. let $\phi$ be the angle between the $x$-axis and the rotator axis.

Question (a)

Find the energy eigenvalues and the corresponding eigenfunctions.

Answer (a)

Suppose $\mu$ is the reduced mass of the rotor in the center of mass reference frame and $r$ is the distance of them. We can say $$ I = \mu r^2. $$ Set the Hamiltonian $H$ such that $$ \hat H=-\frac{\hbar^{2}}{2\mu}\nabla^2. $$ On spherical polar coortinate Laplacian $\nabla^2$ can be described by $$ \nabla^2 f=\frac{1}{r^2}\frac{\partial}{\partial r}\left(r^2\frac {\partial f}{\partial r}\right)+\frac{1}{r^2\sin \theta }\frac{\partial }{\partial \theta }\left(\sin \theta \frac{\partial f}{\partial \theta }\right)+\frac{1}{r^2\sin^2\theta }\frac{\partial^2 f}{\partial \varphi^2} $$ then $$ \hat H=-\frac{\hbar^2}{2I}\left[\frac{1}{\sin \theta }\frac{\partial}{\partial \theta}\left(\sin \theta \frac{\partial}{\partial \theta}\right)+\frac{1}{\sin^2\theta}\frac{\partial^2}{\partial \varphi^2}\right]. $$ Solving the Schrödinger equation $\hat H \Psi =E\Psi$ looks somewhat difficult. However it's similar as the Hamiltnian of Hydrogen atom $$ \hat H_{\mathrm{Hydrogen}} \psi = -{\frac {\hbar ^{2}}{2\mu }}\left[{\frac {1}{r^{2}}}{\frac {\partial }{\partial r}}\left(r^{2}{\frac {\partial \psi }{\partial r}}\right)+{\frac {1}{r^{2}\sin \theta }}{\frac {\partial }{\partial \theta }}\left(\sin \theta {\frac {\partial \psi }{\partial \theta }}\right)+{\frac {1}{r^{2}\sin ^{2}\theta }}{\frac {\partial ^{2}\psi }{\partial \phi ^{2}}}\right]-{\frac {e^{2}}{4\pi \epsilon _{0}r}} \psi $$ We can casually say $\hat H$ is $r$-eliminated hamiltonian from Hydrogen atom. Hence we can find the eigenfunctions as the spherical harmonics $Y_l^m(\theta, \varphi)$ $$ \Psi = Y_l^m(\theta, \varphi) $$ and the corresponding eigenenergies are $$ E_l = \frac{\hbar^2}{2I}l(l+1) $$

Question (b)

At time $t$ equals zero, the rotator is described by a wave packet $\Psi(0) = A \sin ^2 \phi$. Find $\Psi(t)$ for $t > 0$.

Answer (b)

To solve time-dependent Schrödinger equation $i\hbar \frac{\partial}{\partial t}\Psi(t)=\hat {H}\Psi (t)$ for time-dependent Hamiltonian $\hat{H}$, we get $$ \Psi(t) = \Psi(0) e^{-iEt/ \hbar}. $$ Then the answer is $$ \Psi(t) = A \sin^2 \phi\; e^{-iEt/ \hbar}. $$

Problem 2

A hydrogen atom is in a ${}^2\mathrm{P}_{1/2}$ state with total angular momentum up along the $z$-axis. In this problem, show your computations and reasoning carefully.

Question (a)

With what probability will the electron be found with spin down?

Answer (a)

The electron with spin down cannot be found along $z$-axis. Along $x$ and $y$-axes we can find it with 50%.

Question (b)

COmpute the probability per unit solid angle $P(\theta, \varphi)$ that the electron will be found at spherical angles $\theta, \varphi$ independent of radial distance and spin.

Answer (b)

The configuration is $n\mathrm{p}^1$ ($n \geq 2$). So the angle parts of the wave function is $Y_1^m(\theta, \varphi)$ ($m = 1, 0, -1$). Then $$ P(\theta, \varphi) = Y_1^m(\theta, \varphi)^2 \;\;\;(m = 1, 0, -1) $$
