Question on Rigid Rotor 2

Problem 1

A rigid body with moment of inertia of $I_z$ rotates freely in the $x$-$y$ plane. let $\phi$ be the angle between the $x$-axis and the rotator axis. a

Question (a)

Find the energy eigenvalues and the corresponding eigenfunctions.

Answer (a)

$$ \mathcal{H} = -\frac{\hbar^2}{2I}\frac{\partial^2}{\partial \phi^2} $$ Solving Schroedinger equation $\mathcal{H}\Psi = E\Psi$, we get $$ \Psi_n(x) = \sqrt{\frac{1}{\pi}}\sin\frac{n\phi}{2} \;\; (n=1,2,\cdots) $$ $$ E_n = \frac{\hbar^2 n^2}{8 I} $$

Question (b)

At time $t$ equals zero, the rotator is described by a wave packet $\Psi(0) = A \sin ^2 \phi$. Find $\Psi(t)$ for $t > 0$.

Answer (b)

To solve time-dependent Schrödinger equation $i\hbar \frac{\partial}{\partial t}\Psi(t)=\hat {H}\Psi (t)$ for time-dependent Hamiltonian $\hat{H}$, we get $$ \Psi_n(t) = \Psi_n(0) e^{-iE_nt/ \hbar}. $$ Since $\sin^2 \phi = \frac{1-\cos(2\phi)}{2}$ we know $$ \Psi(0) = \frac{A}{2} - \frac{A}{2}\cos(2\phi) = \frac{A}{2} - \frac{A}{2}\sin(-2\phi+\pi/2) $$ So the wavefunction \Psi(0) is the state of $n=4$. Then we say $$ \Psi(t) = \Psi(0) e^{-i 2 \hbar t/ I} $$

Problem 2

A hydrogen atom is in a ${}^2\mathrm{P}_{1/2}$ state with total angular momentum up along the $z$-axis. In this problem, show your computations and reasoning carefully.

Question (a)

With what probability will the electron be found with spin down?

Answer (a)

The electron with spin down cannot be found along $z$-axis. Along $x$ and $y$-axes we can find it with 50%.

Question (b)

COmpute the probability per unit solid angle $P(\theta, \varphi)$ that the electron will be found at spherical angles $\theta, \varphi$ independent of radial distance and spin.

Answer (b)

The configuration is $n\mathrm{p}^1$ ($n \geq 2$). So the angle parts of the wave function is $Y_1^m(\theta, \varphi)$ ($m = 1, 0, -1$). Then $$ P(\theta, \varphi) = Y_1^m(\theta, \varphi)^2 \;\;\;(m = 1, 0, -1) $$
